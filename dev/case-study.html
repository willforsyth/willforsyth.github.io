Money Guru | Client overview

Money guru is a comparison site that went from 0 - 1 million users in its first 6 months and became the fasted growing comparison website in the UK in 2017. I lead the user centric approach to research, design and iterating the product ensuring we continually monitor and improve the experience.

I am going to give a briff overview of the initial release and then focus on a later release to show a detailed process.

0.1 | UX Research

  • Competitor research confused.com, gocompare.com etc
  • Analytics research we had a client site called ukloans to base gather our analytic data from. Not the best but this was enough to start until we had a MVP.
  • Surveys and questionnaires we added hotjar to the site to gather as much data we could on the users behaviours. This monitored all ttypes of user interaction and allowed us to add surveys etc to the site.
  • User stories we wrote over 100 user stories to delve into what the users needs were.
  • User personas we then selected some key user personas to study more and find out what issues and goals they have.

0.2 | UX analysis

  • User story maps helped us to view the whole project and detail how each persona would achieve their goals.
  • Card sorting helped us to prioritise these features.
  • Affinity diagrams we used affinity digrams to group content and define sections.

0.3 | UX design

  • User flows helped us detail each interaction for each personas journey to its completion and discover how they achieved their goals.
  • Interaction design helped us detail each interaction for each personas journey to its completion and discover how they achieved their goals.
  • Wireframes helped us detail each interaction for each personas journey to its completion and discover how they achieved their goals.

0.4 | Prototype

  • Sketch helped us detail each interaction for each personas journey to its completion and discover how they achieved their goals.
  • Marvel app helped us detail each interaction for each personas journey to its completion and discover how they achieved their goals.
  • Principle we also created interactive animated prototypes to demonstrate how the animations and flows would work.

0.5 | UI design

Using the style guide and branding guidelines high fidelity designs were created.

0.6 | Usability testing

  • Lab usability test We found a few issues and some easy wins. The smart search was not offering enough benefits and was hard to understand. The navigation could be more direct.

Money Guru | Phase 2 release 2

A user benefit was spotted within one of the usability tests. This surround the car loan section of the site. There was a lot of visits to this section of the comparison table however not much completion.

It was decided to focus on a new section to appeal to these users.

1.1 | UX Research

The research phase follows the same process as the earlier phase but we now have more information to work with since we can analysis the new site.

What we used

  • Competitor research confused.com, gocompare.com etc
  • Analytics research ...
  • Surveys and questionnaires ...
  • User stories ...
  • User personas we had some real personas now to work with

1.2 | UX analysis

We analysed the data and using a User story map and affinity diagrams we started to build up a nice understanding of what we would like to include.

Findings

  • Car finance we would call it car finance and it would be it's on dedicated section.
  • Comparison table unique comparison table that can filter by car registration plates.
  • Quick quote, Vehicle valuation, Dedicated wisdom content, Car finance calculator, Benefits section, Email / Save results, Car cost estimator, Garage and car search

User story map

This gave us the full picture of the feature. We had one backbone of the big activity, the one under are the steps for each activity to be completed and then under are the details in each step.

We could then prioritise features into releases

  • Must have Quick quote, Dedicated wisdom content, Benefits section, Comparison table
  • Should have Vehicle valuation, Car finance calculator, Email / Save results
  • Could have Garage and car search, Car cost estimator

Not we had the "must have's" it's time to revisit the personas and create user flows to plan out every point of contact.
