WhoCanFixMyCar | Reduce Leakage:

As a product team, we have as one of our main objectives to increase conversion on our funnel. This takes the shape off:

  • 1) Top of funnel - Number of users
  • 2) Posting rate on the form
  • 3) Number of quotes
  • 4) Acceptance rate on the quotes
  • 5) Returning users

One of the areas of leakage we have identified in the funnel is receiving a quote from a garage and then clicking choose a garage.

We approached drivers through our customer contact team who asked the reasons for not using the system to book in, also results from the remote usability tests, to define pain points in the process and work out from the findings what a potential improvement would be.

Based on the remote testing sessions and speaking to some drivers we could start to put together a user journey map that helped us narrow the journey down into some pain points and areas we could improve upon.

Customer journey map

Findings

Feelings: Frustrated, worried about price, unsure

Thoughts: Not sure how to book in, how long will the job take, do I need a courtesy car, why am I getting phone calls from garages.

Drivers call the garage to book in as there is no functionality to book in through the system. Introduce a booking system for the main funnel flow.

Problem statement

“As a driver I would like to book an appointment with the garage easily and quickly."

Research Phase

Now that we have identified that booking system to improve the conversion on accepts and improve the user journey, I set out to gather research on how garages currently book cars in, how they would like to take bookings from us.

Garage visits

We went on the road to gather research into how garages currently book drivers in once they have had a job accepted. We visited 4 garages and spoke to the mechanics and office admin who used the system to talk to drivers and book them in.

Surveys

We have access to all active user of the system through our dashboard, so we can quickly run surveys to gather feedback.

Results

We gathered all the results and held a session on the best way to approach the problem:

  • Garages use multiple systems to manage bookings
  • Some use pen and paper
  • Most will not turn away bookings
  • They accept a specific number of jobs per day, this could change depending on the type of job
  • Some have visited and some have drop-offs

We decided on a MVP would meet our objectives and be used gather feedback on then iterate. This was based on business objectives and also on the drivers and a prioritisation matrix.

  • Allow morning or afternoon drop-offs
  • Allow the garage to set the number of drop-offs they will take per day
  • Allow for overbookings (Business objective)
