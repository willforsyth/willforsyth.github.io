Instant quotes

Problem statement: “Give drivers an instant and accurate quote”

Provide instant accurate prices to drivers, allowing garages to view and edit their quotes at any time.

My Role: Lead Product Designer

  • User research
  • UX design
  • UI design
  • Interaction design

The task

Driver feedback

“What happens now? Do I need to wait on a quote?”

Garage feedback

“I don't have time to quote on the jobs”

Business goal

We need to give the drivers instant prices to increase the acceptance rate and leakage. Give 86% of jobs posted 5+ instant accurate price (Currently 35%) and to increase acceptance rate to 20%

Empathise

Our users expect to see an instant accurate price, and we also have feedback from mechanics who would like to be able to quote quicker. To start will I needed to delve deeper into these needs so I could fully understand the problem.

User journey map

Garage interviews

I interviewed around 20 garages and mechanics.

Findings

Feelings: Concerned that we provide the wrong price, worried about bad experiences with drivers around pricing.

Thoughts: looking forward to saving time with quoting but hoping their is not more customer management.

The main concerns for the garages are around price and making sure these are accurate.

Driver

Do drivers expect the price to be final?

Findings

Drivers expect the quotes to change when they get to the garage depending on whether more more is required, or parts prices etc.

Considerations at this point.

Will mechanics be happy with us quoting on their behalf?

This is a business goal so will be happening we need to ensure that the data is accurate so they are happy with the quotes.

Will Drivers accept a “Smart quote” or fully understand what it is.

This will be a research piece, for the usability research team.

Define

I gathered all the results and held a session consisting of affinity diagrams to group the results and decide on the best way to approach the problem:

  • Drivers expect prices to move, however we need to ensure they know its a quote
  • Garages want accurate prices:
  • The AI has a tolerance of plus and minus 10% so we need to give the the power to manage and update prices.
  • The parts plus labour calculations do not take into account the "Fudge factor". For example a certain model ford focus may not need a part removed to get itn othe timing belt.
  • Garages need to update prices instantly and live.
  • Garages need a management tool to view there prices and to update if they feel they need to. (They will have upwards of 300,000 potential quotes)

Defined MVP for the AI solution

Myself, the product manager and the data scientist then decided on a MVP to go live with. This was based on when we could have the first cut of data, how quickly we could test a prototype and how much management we want to give to the drivers on the release.

  • Onboarding for the mechanics
  • New quote tiles, tooltips, more info sections as well as email coms
  • Management tool that allows the mechanic to view all prices and update

Design / Ideate

I then set out designing the flows, wireframes, interaction designs and prototypes, so we could test and iterate.

Sketch and plan full end to end flow

I always start out with sketches and rough flows and wireframes as I work out the possible routes. The flow touches the mechanics and driver in numurous places. So will need several flows ranging in detail and then several prototypes.

Usability test

This is the first point to gather some usability feedback on thoughts and perceptions of the branding and how this would work. I could not test the full flow at this point as emails and texts messages, updating tiles etc would take a lot of dev to test this was to gather as much as I could from static wireframe click throughs.
